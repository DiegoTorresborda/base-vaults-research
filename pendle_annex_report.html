Executive Summary

  • A Pendle "market" ≠ a vault — it's a yield-splitting venue. Each market splits a yield-bearing asset (e.g. sUSDe, wstETH) into a PT (Principal Token — buy at a discount, redeem at face at maturity → a fixed yield), a YT (Yield Token), plus an LP position earning swap fees + PEND incentives. The same underlying can have several markets, one per maturity.
  • Overwhelmingly a stablecoin / synthetic-dollar product. 72 of 96 markets ($220.6M, 91% of liquidity) are stablecoin-denominated (USDe, sUSDe, USDat, apyUSD, USD0, reUSDe…). ETH derivatives are 16 markets ($15.0M); BTC just 3 ($4.0M).
  • Ethereum is the home chain. Ethereum holds 80 markets ($176.6M, 73%); Arbitrum 10 ($61.3M); Base only 6 ($5.6M). Median PT fixed yield: ETH 7.75%, Arbitrum 7.02%, Base 9.86%.
  • Fixed yields run 5–24%, well above the vault census. PT implied APY clusters 5–10% on liquid stable markets and reaches 12–24% on smaller synthetic dollars (apyUSD 24.2%, apxUSD 18.2%, reUSDe 16.6%) — materially higher than 3–6% blue-chip stable vault yields, as compensation for Pendle, maturity/liquidity, and underlying risk.
  • Thin, short-dated liquidity → no Bajo tier. 57 of 96 markets are sub-$1M and every position carries a maturity date — PT redeems at face only at expiry. This drives the risk profile: no Bajo-tier markets (Medio 39 / $209.0M · Alto 57 / $34.5M).

Risk Notes

per-construction flags · tier distribution

Every Pendle market carries, by construction: centralized_dependency (protocol-managed AMM + price oracle), il_risk (the LP side has impermanent loss vs holding PT/underlying), and — for the pegged-asset markets — depeg_risk. Pendle's core contracts are audited (ChainSecurity, Spearbit, Dedaub, Ackee — Tier 1), so no smart_contract_unaudited flag is applied at the protocol level.

Risk-tier distribution (same additive rubric as the vault reports): Medio 39 markets / $209.0M · Alto 57 markets / $34.5M · Bajo 0. The skew to Alto is driven by (a) the small size of most markets (57 of 96 are sub-$1M → +2 TVL penalty) and (b) the inherent il_risk + depeg_risk + centralized_dependency stack. Larger markets (≥$1M, stable) land in Medio.

1
Maturity risk (every market). A PT is only redeemable at face value at its maturity date. Before maturity it trades on the AMM at a market price; the "fixed APY" assumes hold-to-maturity. Days-to-maturity is in the CSV (days_to_maturity).
2
Liquidity risk. 57 markets are sub-$1M. Thin AMM depth means early exit can incur meaningful slippage, and very small markets may be hard to enter/exit at size.
3
Underlying risk is inherited in full. A Pendle market is a wrapper over an underlying yield source (e.g. Ethena sUSDe, Falcon USDf, a synthetic dollar). All of that underlying's depeg/solvency/smart-contract risk passes through to the PT and LP holder. The high-APY markets (apyUSD, apxUSD, reUSDe) wrap higher-risk synthetic dollars — the yield is risk compensation.
4
PT vs LP are different risk profiles. PT held to maturity ≈ a fixed-income instrument (no IL, defined payoff). LP earns variable fees + PEND incentives but bears IL and yield variability. The il_risk flag reflects the LP side; a buy-and-hold PT investor is not exposed to IL. Both share the underlying and protocol risk.
5
Incentive dependence. Part of the LP return (pendle_reward_apy, lp_max_boosted_apy) is PEND-token incentives that can change; the PT fixed rate does not depend on incentives.

Methodology

Pendle API + on-chain verification

Objective

Catalogue 100% of active Pendle markets on Ethereum, Arbitrum and Base — one row per market — with standardized fixed/variable yields, maturity, risk profile, and verified on-chain addresses.

Data sources

  • Market universe & financials: Pendle official API https://api-v2.pendle.finance/core/v1/{chainId}/markets/active for chainId 1, 42161, 8453 (snapshot 2026-06-05). Fields used: market address, pt/yt/sy token addresses, expiry, underlyingAsset, categoryIds, and details.{liquidity, impliedApy, aggregatedApy, pendleApy, maxBoostedApy, feeRate}.
  • Cross-reference: DeFiLlama yields feed (project == "pendle") confirmed the same 96 markets appear as 192 pool rows (one PT + one LP per market); DeFiLlama's $483.5M is gross (double-counts PT and LP legs), so this annex reports Pendle's net liquidity ($243.5M) instead.
  • Verification: every market address re-checked for non-empty bytecode on its chain's public RPC (ethereum-rpc, arbitrum-one-rpc, base-rpc publicnode). 96/96 passed.

Column definitions (pendle_markets.csv, 23 columns)

chain · market_name (underlying) · category (always fixed_yield) · underlying_asset_class · maturity / days_to_maturity · tvl_usd (Pendle market liquidity) · pt_implied_apy (the fixed yield for a PT buyer held to maturity) · lp_apy (aggregated LP APY) · pendle_reward_apy (PEND incentive component) · lp_max_boosted_apy · fee_rate · risk_flags · risk_score / risk_tier (same rubric as the vault reports) · pendle_categories · is_prime · market_address · pt_address · sy_address · underlying_token · source_url · protocol_tier (Tier 1).

Relationship to the vault censuses

These 96 markets are the same instruments excluded from the Base, Arbitrum and Ethereum vault reports under the "Pendle PT/LP — not deposit-and-earn vaults" exclusion rule. On Ethereum, the largest single Pendle pool (~$12.8M) does not even clear the $25M vault floor; on Arbitrum, 6 Pendle pools were explicitly excluded. This annex is the complete, floor-free accounting of that excluded set.

Limitations

Pendle APYs are point-in-time and especially sensitive to maturity proximity and AMM state. The fixed (PT) APY is only realized on hold-to-maturity with no underlying default. The risk tier is a transparent heuristic, not investment advice. This annex is informational only.
